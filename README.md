**Customer Churn Analysis**



**Overview:**

This project analyzes a dataset of 10,000+ credit card customers to identify key factors associated with customer attrition. The goal is to understand behavioral patterns that indicate churn risk and provide insights for improving customer retention.



**Tools Used:**

\- SQL (data analysis and querying)

\- Excel (data visualization and dashboarding)



**Dataset:**

The dataset contains customer demographic and transactional information, including income category, credit limit, transaction count, and spending behavior.



**Key Questions:**

\- What percentage of customers have churned?

\- How does customer behavior differ between churned and active users?

\- Are certain customer segments more likely to churn?

\- What patterns indicate increased churn risk?



**Key Findings:**

\- Approximately 16% of customers in the dataset have churned

\- Churned customers complete \~35% fewer transactions, spend \~33% less and have \~45% less utilization than active customers

\- Reduced transaction activity and spending are strong indicators of declining engagement prior to churn

\- Certain customer segments show slightly higher churn rates, suggesting demographic factors may influence retention



**Recommendations:**

\- Monitor customers with declining transaction activity and spending

\- Implement targeted retention strategies for low-engagement customers

\- Use behavioral metrics as early warning indicators for churn risk
